The Portland Trail Blazers earned the No. 3 seed in a competitive Western Conference largely through the strength of their backcourt. Damian Lillard and C.J. McCollum combined to average 48.3 points per game this season, the second-best number for a guard combo in the NBA (only James Harden and Chris Paul averaged more).

Lillard in particular has been special. He averaged 26.9 points and 6.6 assists, and he’s a good bet to earn 1st Team All-NBA honors for his efforts.

So far, and it’s early, he’s been absolutely manhandled by New Orleans’ Jrue Holiday.

It starts with defense. Lillard has averaged 17.5 points through two first-round playoff games, down 9.4 from his regular season average -- despite taking 1.1 more shots per game in this series. You could chalk this up to gameplanning or strong team defense, but according to NBA.com, Lillard has shot 2-for-18 when Holiday guards him (11.1 percent) and 11-for-23 (47.8 percent) when everyone else does.

But Holiday isn’t just a low-usage defensive stopper in the mold of Andre Roberson or Luc Mbah-a-Moute. No, the New Orleans guard averaged a career-high 19 points and 6 assists this season, and he’s kicked it up another notch in the postseason. Right now, he’s averaging 27 points per game on 54.5 percent shooting -- better on both counts than either of Portland’s guards have mustered.
